About Me

I am Yenson Lau, a machine learning scientist and engineer. Having grown up in Hong Kong and Vancouver, I spent most of my life and career in diverse urban environments. I am currently based in Toronto.

Until recently I was at Layer 6 AI, developing models for TD Bank and performing research on graph neural networks for drug repurposing as well as personalized federated learning. Prior to this I investigated optimization methods for deconvolution as a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical Engineering at Columbia University (currently withdrawn, ABD).

I am currently spending a lot of time learning about transformer models in natural language processing, as well as the challenging tasks and applications that these models are bringing into reach. I am also working on a side project investigating the behavior of BERT models when pretraining is subject to PII remediation, and am eager to work with language models further at my next role, ideally in an applied research setting.

Outside of work, I love to travel and read. My most recent recommendations are Range: Why Generalists Triumph in a Specialized World by David Epstein, and Awareness by Anthony de Mello.

For inquiries and collaboration, please reach out to me on LinkedIn. 🙂
